Europe

It is the second smallest continent in the world composed of the peninsulas of Eurasia.
It takes up a fifteeth of the world's total land and shares
its north border with the Arctic Ocean, west border with the Atlantic Ocean,
and the south border with the Mediterranean Sea,
the Black Sea, the Kuma-Manych Depression, and the Caspian Sea.
Sources: Britanicca
Image: Hiki Liu - Unsplash






































Asia

It is the largest and most diversified continent in the world.
The term Asia only refers to the landmass and cannot
be used to homogenise the people who exist in the continent as it is so diverse.
Asia also has the world's most diverse flaura and fauna.
Sources: Britanicca
Image: Stephen Leonardi - Unsplash






































North America

The northern portion is occupied by North America.
It is often called the New World or simply the Americas. The mainland is shaped almost like a triangle
with the top being wider than its base. It is associated with Greenland, the largest island in the world,
and such offshore groups as the Arctic Archipelago.
Sources: Britanicca
Image: Aditya Chinchure - Unsplash






































South America

South America shares its borders with the the
Caribbean Sea in the northwest and north,
the Atlantic Ocean in the northeast, east, and southeast,
and the Pacific Ocean to the west. In the northwest,
it is joined to North America by the Isthmus of Panama.
Sources: Britanicca
Image: Vanessa Bumbeers - Unsplash






































Africa

Africa is the second largest continet in the world.
The continent shares its borders on the west with the Atlantic Ocean,
on the north with the Mediterranean Sea,
on the east with the Red Sea and the Indian Ocean, and on the south
with the the Atlantic and Indian oceans.
Sources: Britanicca
Image: Tebogo Losaba - Unsplash






































Australia

Australia is both a country and a continent. It is the
smallest continent yet the largest country in the world and lies between the Pacific and
Indian oceans in the Southern Hemisphere. Australia’s capital Canberra is located in the southeast
between the larger and more important centres of Sydney and Melbourne.
Sources: Britanicca
Image: Dan Freeman - Unsplash
