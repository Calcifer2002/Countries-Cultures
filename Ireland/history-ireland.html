History of Ireland

Here is a brief history: The first settlers came to Ireland around 8000 BC. These people were called hunter-gatherers as they hunted meat and fish while also gathering berries for food. Around 4000 BC, people began to farm. Around 3,200 BC, descendants of these people went on to build the famous passage grave known as Newgrange. Amidst this, we had the stone age first and then the bronze age. Then came the iron age which brought the famous Celts. These were famous for their fighting and also their jewellery. Around 432 AD, St. Patrick came to Ireland and converted the pagan Irish to Christianity. The monks came to Ireland around 500 AD. They built well known monasteries such as the one in Glendalough. Vikings came to Ireland around 795 AD. They settled along the east coast around Dublin and Wicklow. Known as the “pale” back then. They were feared by many and were very skilled craftsmen. The Normans then invaded Ireland in the 12th century..

Many famous people were present in this period such as Strongbow. The Norman times are a huge part of the history in Ireland but I hope to add some more detail another time to this website. The 17th century saw the O’Neills and the O’Donnells fight off the English armies sent by Queen Elizabeth to take over Ulster ( The nine years war). The famous plantations and presence of Oliver Cromwell also occurred in this century. In 1803, a rebellion led by Robert Emmet against the English for freedom and independence for the Irish. We then had the Famine from 1845-1850. This was caused by blight and killed a huge part of the population due to hunger and starvation. Many left for America on “coffin ships”. The 20th century saw the fight for independence for the irish. To be a free state. This led to the 1916 rising. Arguably the most important piece of history in ireland
