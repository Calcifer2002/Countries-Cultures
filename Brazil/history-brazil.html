History of Brazil

Brazil was originally thought to have been occupied by Asians thousands of years ago. The country was then discovered by the Portuguese and because of the abundance of good land, they cultivated sugarcane. For this slaves were brought from Africa as slavery was still not considered an issue way back. Soon enough diamond and gold was discovered in the country which further increased the reasons for the Portuguese to stay there. The Portuguese had actually tried to first enslave the Indians who lived there but failed because many could not survive the European diseases and were not used to long hours of hard work. When it was first discovered the place had 30 million indigenous people living there but now lesser than 30,000 remain. A shocking reduction. The country even received its name from the colonizers because they wanted sole control of pau-brasil, a kind of redwood tree used for making red dyes. Most of the colonialists preferred to live along the coastal lines but some still went into the hinterlands. These were the Jesus missionaries who wanted to convert more people or people in search of slaves.
