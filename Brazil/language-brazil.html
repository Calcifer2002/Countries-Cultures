Languages of Brazil

Due to colonialists, Brazil's language is Portuguese. However it differs a lot from the European version of the language mainly due to influences of indigenous languages, and other colonialists who tried to occupy Brazil. This form of Portuguese is called Brazilian Portuguese and differs very highly in the lexicons and phonetics compared to its European cousin. Even within Brazilian Portuguese one can find differences in pronunciations in different regions because of the influence of people who migrated there.


English Brazilian Portugese
Good morning Bom dia (boa tarde)
Good evening Boa noite
How are you? Como vai você?
Well, thank you Bem, obrigado
Hello (to answer the telephone) Alô
Hello (common forms of greeting) Bom dia, boa tarde, etc.
My name is (I am) Meu nome é (Eu sou)
It’s a pleasure é um prazer
