Languages of Australia

Australia's de facto national language is English, even though it is not the official language. Nevertheless, there are almost 200 Aboriginal languages, many of which have been extinct since 1950, and most of which have very few speakers left. Approximately 8,000 Mabuiag speakers live in the Western Torres Strait Islands, and 4,000 Western Desert participants speak the Western Desert language. About 50,000 Aboriginal individuals know the Australian language. Australians also speak the languages of immigrants, including Greek, Italian, and Chinese.
