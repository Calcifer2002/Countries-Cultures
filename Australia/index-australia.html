Introduction

The sixth-largest country on Earth is Australia. There are 12 million square kilometers of ocean territory in the country, spread across three oceans. Indigenous vegetation occupies nearly seven million square kilometers, or 91 percent, of Australia's territory. Despite this high number, many of Australia's desert landscapes are dominated by native plants such as saltbush. With some of the world's oldest land, Australia's soils and seas, despite their biodiversity, rank among the least productive and least nutrient-rich in the world.
