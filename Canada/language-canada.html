Languages of Canada

As part of the Canadian constitution, French and English are recognized as official languages. English, however, is the majority language throughout most of Canada. Only New Brunswick is considered bilingual, and French is the official language in only Quebec, where French is spoken as a first language by four in five people. An estimated half to three-quarters of Canadians identify their first language as English; about one-fifth identify their primary language as French. About one-fifth of Canadians speak another language besides English or French, while the majority of immigrants speak Chinese, as Chinese immigration has surged since the 1980s. Inuit have a variety of dialects of Inuktitut, their language. Among the indigenous languages, the most widespread is Cree.
