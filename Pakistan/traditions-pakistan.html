Traditions of Pakistan

Pashtuns traditions consist of Pashtunwali, religion and attire. Pakhtunwali primarily can be described as the code of life and honour which every Pashtun should follow. It comprises principles which should be observed by every Pashtun. The main principles are:

  • Hospitality
  • Protection of the weaks and vulnerable
  • Honour
  • Loyalty
  • Respect
  • Love
  • Nation

Pashtuns mainly followed religion of Islam. Religion plays a vital role in their lives and it is mainly what influences their way of life. Pashtuns are practicing muslims and follow sunni Islam except a few tribes who are shia muslims at the wester border of Pakistan near Pakistan-Iran border. Pashtun wear their traditional dresses which are called Partug-Kamzeez. The attire are basically long shirts reached until knees and loose trousers for both men and women. However, it's in a different style for both men and women. Men wear it in plain colours and style while women wear it in multiple colours with embroidery on it.
